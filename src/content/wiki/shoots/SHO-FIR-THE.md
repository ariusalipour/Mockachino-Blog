The Smith & Wesson M&P 15-22 is a popular semi-automatic rifle chambered in .22 LR, designed for practical shooting competitions and plinking. It’s a lightweight, affordable, and reliable rifle that has gained a lot of popularity among gun enthusiasts. However, while it is a great rifle in many ways, it also has some drawbacks that make it the best and worst practical mini rifle.

Let’s start with the positive aspects of the M&P 15-22. Firstly, it is an affordable rifle that is great for beginners or those on a budget. It’s considerably cheaper than many other rifles in its class, and its .22 LR chambering makes it cheaper to shoot as well.

Secondly, the M&P 15-22 is a very accurate rifle. It’s great for plinking or target shooting, and it’s also a popular choice for practical shooting competitions. Its lightweight design and adjustable stock make it easy to handle and shoot accurately.

Thirdly, the M&P 15-22 is very versatile. It has a Picatinny rail system that allows for the easy attachment of accessories such as scopes, red dots, and other tactical gear. The rifle also has a collapsible stock, making it easy to store and transport.

However, there are also some drawbacks to the M&P 15-22 that make it the worst practical mini rifle. Firstly, the rifle’s construction can feel somewhat cheap. The polymer lower and upper receivers can be a turn off for some users who prefer the feel of metal.

Secondly, the M&P 15-22 can be sensitive to ammunition. Some users have reported issues with the rifle jamming or failing to cycle with certain types of ammunition. While this can be an issue with any rifle, it seems to be more common with the M&P 15-22.

Finally, the M&P 15-22 can be prone to some reliability issues. While it is generally a reliable rifle, some users have reported issues with the rifle’s bolt catch, which can prevent the rifle from firing. This can be a major issue in practical shooting competitions where every shot counts.

In conclusion, the Smith & Wesson M&P 15-22 is both the best and worst practical mini rifle. Its accuracy, affordability, and versatility make it a great choice for beginners and those on a budget, as well as those who want a lightweight and accurate rifle for plinking or practical shooting competitions. However, its sensitivity to ammunition, reliability issues, and somewhat cheap construction can be a turn off for some users.
