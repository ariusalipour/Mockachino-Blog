While many competitors begin their IPSC journey with a handgun, the skills learned in other disciplines can be easily transferred to the sport. This is especially so for people like me who do not have easy access to handguns, and hence need to use alternatives such as IPSC Action Air, which is an airsoft-based version of IPSC.

IPSC Action Air is a great way to train for IPSC competitions. The guns used in IPSC Action Air are airsoft replicas of real firearms, which means that they are affordable to shoot and provide a realistic shooting experience. Additionally, IPSC Action Air courses of fire are designed to mimic IPSC handgun stages, which allows competitors to train for the same scenarios they will face in a live match.

One of the biggest benefits of training with IPSC Action Air is the ability to practice movement and shooting on the move. IPSC Action Air courses often require competitors to navigate through obstacles while engaging targets, which is an important skill for any practical shooting competition. Additionally, since the guns used in IPSC Action Air are lightweight and easy to manipulate, competitors can practice quickly drawing and acquiring targets, which is also a critical skill in IPSC handgun competitions.

Another benefit of IPSC Action Air training is the ability to train in the comfort of your own home. Since airsoft guns are much quieter than firearms, you can set up a course of fire in your backyard or even in your living room without disturbing your neighbors. This makes it easier to practice regularly, which is important for improving your skills and technique.

While training with IPSC Action Air is a great way to prepare for IPSC handgun competitions, it’s important to remember that the two disciplines are not identical. The guns used in IPSC Action Air have different trigger pulls, recoil patterns, and sight pictures than their live-fire counterparts, so it’s important to continue training with a real firearm as well. However, by supplementing your live-fire training with IPSC Action Air practice, you can improve your skills and technique and better prepare yourself for your next IPSC competition.

In conclusion, training with IPSC Action Air is an excellent way to prepare for IPSC handgun competitions. The skills learned in IPSC Action Air, such as movement and shooting on the move, are easily transferable to IPSC handgun competitions. Additionally, the affordability and ease of training with IPSC Action Air make it an accessible and convenient training option for any shooter looking to improve their practical shooting skills.
